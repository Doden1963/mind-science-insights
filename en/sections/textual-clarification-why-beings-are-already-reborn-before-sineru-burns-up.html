🔹 TEXTUAL CLARIFICATION – why beings are already reborn before Sineru burns up 🔹

World cycles, rebirth, heavens, mahākappa, and conclusion.

DanskSee this section inside full textBack to category

• In the very earliest Buddhist texts (the Nikāya canon), world-dissolution (saṃvaṭṭa) is described as a slow, cosmic process, not as a sudden catastrophe.
🔥 What is described – and what is not?
📜 Aṅguttara Nikāya 7.62 – Sattasūriya Sutta (The Seven Suns)
Here it says:
“Ayaṃ mahāpathavī ca Sineru ca pabbatarājā
ādittā bhavissanti, sampajjalitā, ekā jalitā bhavissanti.”
• “This great earth and Sineru, king of mountains, will be ignited, blaze up, and become one burning mass.” 🔥
And further:
“Seyyathāpi sappi vā telaṃ vā anupādisesaṃ pariyādāya jhāyeyya…”
• “Just as clarified butter or oil burns completely without ash or soot.”
➡️ Crucial observation:
• ❌ Nothing is said about:
– sattā (beings) dying
– pāṇa (living creatures) perishing
– maraṇa (death) of humans, animals, or gods
📌 In Pāli texts, the death of beings is always mentioned explicitly when it is relevant.
👉 The fact that it is not mentioned here is therefore meaningful.
🧠 The textual conclusion
• When sattā (beings) are not mentioned while the whole physical structure is destroyed
• this means, according to a textual reading and modern research:
➡️ there are already no beings left in this world-system.
That is:
• punabbhava (rebirth) has already ceased locally
• beings have already died in their previous lives
• and have been reborn elsewhere in accordance with kamma (karma)
🔄 World-dissolution as process – not total annihilation
📜 Dīgha Nikāya 1 – Brahmajāla Sutta
“Saṃvaṭṭati loko, vivaṭṭati loko.”
• “The world contracts, the world expands.”
➡️ No element-mechanics are mentioned
➡️ No total annihilation of all life
➡️ Only gradual cessation of conditions
🌌 Other worlds exist at the same time
📜 Majjhima Nikāya 49 – Brahma-nimantanika Sutta
“Atthi kho, brahme, añño loko.”
• “There are other worlds, Brahmā.”
➡️ When one cakkavāḷa (world-system) ceases:
• others still exist
• beings are reborn there
• not through physical movement
• but through karmic rebirth (cuti–paṭisandhi)
🔄 Cosmic cycles without the end of life
📜 Majjhima Nikāya 4 – Bhayabherava Sutta
“Anekepi saṃvaṭṭavivaṭṭakappe anussarāmi.”
• “I recollect many aeons of world-contraction and expansion.”
➡️ Beings are reborn across these cycles
➡️ No cosmic pause is described in which all life ceases
🔥 Only fire in the earliest Buddhism
• In the Nikāya texts there is only:
– 🔥 destruction by fire (tejo-saṃvaṭṭa)
• ❌ No udaka-saṃvaṭṭa (water)
• ❌ No vāta-saṃvaṭṭa (wind)
➡️ Water and wind appear only in later Abhidhamma and commentarial traditions.
🧭 The crucial distinction
• 🔥 Sineru’s destruction:
– one local cakkavāḷa ceases
• 🌌 Not the whole universe
• 🧠 Sattā are already reborn elsewhere
• 🚫 No physical “evacuation”
• ✅ Only kamma and the direction of mind determine “where to”
🕊️ Only Nibbāna (nibbānaṃ) is the end of the whole cycle (saṃsāra).
✨ In short
• The fact that the death of beings is not mentioned means:
👉 they are already reborn elsewhere (punabbhava)
• Sineru burns later 🔥
• Life has already left the system 🌌
This is directly textual, documented in Pāli, and in accordance with modern research on the very earliest Buddhism.
