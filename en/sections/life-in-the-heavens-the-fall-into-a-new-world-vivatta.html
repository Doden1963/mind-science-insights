👼 Life in the heavens + the “fall” into a new world (vivatta)

Transport between planes of existence, close textual reading, world dissolution, mahākappa, and heavens.

Dansk See this section inside full text Back to category

👼 Life in the heavens is far better than life on Earth
The higher heavens (Ābhassara, Subhakiṇṇa, Vehapphala, and the arūpa worlds) are described as places with:

• no disease

• no hunger

• subtle or immaterial bodies

• jhāna bliss

• enormously long lives

• no conflicts

• great degrees of peace, light, and clarity

The Buddha says that even the highest earthly happiness cannot compare.

🔥 The fate of beings in the fire-destruction during cosmic contraction

• Humans, animals, hell-beings, and lower gods perish with their realm.

• Beings up to Ābhassara may rise to higher heavens or be reborn later.

• The highest heavens (above Vehapphala, including the arūpa worlds) always survive.

• Since there are countless world-systems, beings can be reborn in other universes not undergoing dissolution.

🌱 The beginning of a new world (vivatta) – the “fall”
When a world unfolds:

• The luminous Ābhassara devas come first.

• They are self-luminous, without sex, without gross bodies, without need for food.

• Some taste the earth’s nourishment → lose their light → the body becomes heavier.

• Differences arise → pride, envy, conflict.

This is the “fall” as in the Aggañña Sutta.
