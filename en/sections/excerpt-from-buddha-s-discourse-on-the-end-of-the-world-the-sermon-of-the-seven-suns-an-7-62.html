🔥 Excerpt from “Buddha's Discourse on the End of the World” / “The Sermon of the Seven Suns” (AN 7.62)

Early Buddhism, scholars, the few cosmological statements, and Sineru.

Dansk See this section inside full text Back to category

Source:
https://obo.genaud.net/dhamma-vinaya/misc/an/07_sevens/an07.062.edmn.misc.htm
“After a final, inconceivably long period, a seventh sun appears. And then, monks, this great earth and Sineru, king of mountains, will blaze up, flare, and become one mass of fire.
As the earth and the mountains burn and are consumed, a spark is carried by the wind and taken all the way up to the worlds of the gods. The peaks of Mount Sineru, burning, engulfed, and destroyed, collapse as one enormous mass of fire and crumble over a hundred — indeed, five hundred — yojanas.
And when this great earth and Sineru, king of mountains, have been wholly consumed and burned up, neither ash nor soot will remain. Just as when clarified butter or oil burns up completely, monks, and there is no trace of soot or ash, so it is with the great earth and Mount Sineru.”
