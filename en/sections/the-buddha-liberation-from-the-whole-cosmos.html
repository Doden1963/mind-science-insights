🧘 🥇 The Buddha – liberation from the whole cosmos

World-axes, humanistic rankings, and Plato and the Buddha.

DanskSee this section inside full textBack to category

Humanism: very high
The Buddha’s starting point is existential:
• All conditioned things are suffering (dukkha)
• All is impermanent (anicca)
• No eternal soul (anattā)
The goal is not a better place in the cosmos, but an exit from the whole cycle of worlds.
Central features:
• No creator god
• No absolute metaphysical authority
• All beings can reach liberation
• Even gods are subject to impermanence
• Cosmos is secondary – the ending of suffering is primary
Nibbāna is not a heaven, but:
• cessation of craving
• cessation of rebirth
• cessation of cosmic bondage
➡️ The Buddha offers a universal, non-hierarchical path out of the entire system of existence.
