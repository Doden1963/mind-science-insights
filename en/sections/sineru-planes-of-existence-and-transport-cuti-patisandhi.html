🧘‍♂️ Sineru, planes of existence, and “transport” (cuti–paṭisandhi)

World cycles, rebirth, heavens, mahākappa, and conclusion.

DanskSee this section inside full textBack to category

In early Buddhism, it is described that the Buddha and certain monks could move between planes of existence, not as physical journeys in space, but through states of consciousness attained in deep meditation.
The enormous measurements, ring mountains, and layered worlds came only in later cosmological systems.
👉 “In the oldest layers, Sineru is used to show that even the cosmic center in a world-system will one day perish – not because the whole universe ceases, but because all conditioned worlds are subject to impermanence.”
🌌 Cosmic worlds and “transport” between planes of existence in Buddhism
One may possibly infer from early Buddhism that the heavenly planes belonging to this world-system will be destroyed when the sun in a distant future engulfs the Earth and this whole cosmic system dissolves.
But at the same time, Buddhism teaches that there are countless other world-systems with their own planes of existence, which are not dissolving at the same time.
The question then is how beings are “transported” to these other worlds.
According to the oldest Buddhist texts, no physical journey through space and time occurs. The transition takes place through rebirth via consciousness and karma.
• When a being dies (cuti), a new consciousness (paṭisandhi) arises immediately in another plane of existence, determined by the state of mind and previous actions.
• No intermediate journey and no movement through space are described – only an immediate continuity of consciousness, like a flame lighting a new flame.
• Thus beings leave this world-system long before its physical dissolution and are reborn in other worlds in accordance with their karma.
