🌍 Cosmological background + Mahākappa (4 phases)

World cycles, rebirth, heavens, mahākappa, and conclusion.

DanskSee this section inside full textBack to category

The universe undergoes cycles of:
• expansion / unfolding (vivatta)
• duration
• contraction / destruction (samvatta)
• an empty period before a new unfolding
There are:
• thousandfold world-systems
• thousand-times-thousandfold
• and “great thousandfold world-systems”
One full cycle is called a mahākappa.
The Buddha said: If one had a mountain 14 km high and stroked it with a silk cloth once every 100 years, the mountain would wear away faster than a mahākappa would end.
🌌 1. What is a mahākappa?
In early Buddhism, a mahākappa (great aeon) is the longest conceivable cosmic unit of time.
A mahākappa consists of four immense periods, which together make up one full cosmic cycle:
• samvaṭṭa – dissolution of the world (contraction / destruction)
• samvaṭṭa-ṭṭhāyin – empty intermediate period after dissolution
• vivaṭṭa – re-arising / unfolding of the world (expansion)
• vivaṭṭa-ṭṭhāyin – stable period in which the world endures
Thus:
👉 A mahākappa = dissolution → emptiness → new unfolding → stable existence
Not just one contraction and one expansion – but also:
• a long empty phase without physical world
• and a long stable phase afterward
🧭 2. Is this only one universe or many?
Very importantly:
• A mahākappa applies to one world-system (one cakkavāḷa).
But there are:
• countless world-systems
• which are not necessarily in the same phase at the same time
So:
👉 When one world is dissolving
👉 others are in a stable phase
👉 and others are in a new unfolding
It is a genuine multiverse system.
🔥 3. World-dissolution is not a single event
In the texts, samvaṭṭa (dissolution) is described as:
• extremely slow
• stepwise
• happening over inconceivably long times
And it can happen in three ways (in later tradition):
• by fire (tejo-saṃvaṭṭa)
• by water (udaka-saṃvaṭṭa)
• by wind (vāta-saṃvaṭṭa)
But in the oldest layers, only fire is mentioned.
That means:
👉 Dissolution is not one catastrophe, but a long cosmic process.
🌌 4. Relation to Big Bounce cosmology
Big Bounce models typically have:
• expansion
• contraction
• bounce
• new expansion
But Buddhism has a richer structure:
1. stable world (vivaṭṭa-ṭṭhāyin)
2. dissolution (samvaṭṭa)
3. empty phase without world (samvaṭṭa-ṭṭhāyin)
4. new unfolding (vivaṭṭa)
→ then again stable phase
Thus:
👉 Not just bounce, but also a long “cosmic emptiness” between universes.
This closely resembles modern ideas of:
• “ekpyrotic phase”
• “pre-big-bang vacuum”
• “quantum vacuum state”
🧠 5. The Buddha says directly that he experienced many mahākappas
In e.g. MN 4 and MN 36 the Buddha says:
“I recollected many aeons of world-contraction
many aeons of world-expansion
many aeons of both movements…”
This is extremely important:
He does not merely say:
• many lives
but:
• many cosmic cycles
Thus:
👉 Samsara extends across countless universes through many mahākappas.
