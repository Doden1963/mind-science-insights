🧠 Consciousness and the brain as a possible “antenna” (hypothesis)

Transport between planes of existence, close textual reading, world dissolution, mahākappa, and heavens.

Dansk See this section inside full text Back to category

I also think it cannot be ruled out that other real planes of existence exist, and that the brain in a certain sense can function like an antenna receiving information from them.
Perhaps this happens through very small quantum events in strong fields across extremely short distances, which are found everywhere in the brain. In that case, consciousness is not only something created locally in the neurons, but also something that, to a limited extent, can receive information from a larger cosmic or non-local level.
One possible motivation for this thought is that both animals and humans solve complex open-ended problems every day that no one has yet taught artificial intelligence to solve in the same way without such an “antenna.”
Examples include:

• driving a car safely from A to B in unpredictable traffic situations

• using hands and arms to fold clothes neatly

• grasping objects with precise force and timing

• navigating intuitively in complex environments

These tasks require:

• continuous sensing

• rapid holistic understanding

• bodily coordination

• flexible problem-solving

and are often solved without conscious calculation.
It is therefore tempting to speculate that consciousness is not exclusively a closed system in the brain, but may also have access to a broader information field not yet fully understood by neuroscience or artificial intelligence.
