🟢 The earliest ideas about planes of existence in Buddhism

Transport between planes of existence, close textual reading, world dissolution, mahākappa, and heavens.

Dansk See this section inside full text Back to category

According to modern scholars, the early Buddha taught that consciousness can be reborn in many different forms of existence – humans, animals, heavenly worlds, and worlds of suffering. These worlds were understood as real, but not eternal. No god judges – karma and the direction of the mind determine rebirth.
Even the highest heavenly realms are impermanent. Therefore, the Buddha pointed to one exit lying beyond all worlds: Nibbāna.
