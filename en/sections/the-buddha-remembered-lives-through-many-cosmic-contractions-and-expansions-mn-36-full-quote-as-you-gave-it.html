🧘 The Buddha remembered lives through many cosmic contractions and expansions (MN 36 – full quote as you gave it)

World cycles, rebirth, heavens, mahākappa, and conclusion.

DanskSee this section inside full textBack to category

Big Bounce cosmology: Majjhima Nikaya 4.
https://suttacentral.net/mn4/en/bodhi?lang=en&reference=none&highlight=false
“Once my concentrated mind was purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilement, pliant, workable, steady, and imperturbable, I directed it to the knowledge of recollecting past lives. I recollected my many past lives, namely: one birth, two births, three births, four births, five births, ten births, twenty births, thirty births, forty births, fifty births, one hundred births, one thousand births, one hundred thousand births, many aeons where the world slowly contracted, many aeons where the world slowly unfolded again, many aeons where these two movements followed one another in inconceivably long cosmic cycles:
‘There I was with such a name, of such a clan, with such an appearance; this was my food, such was my experience of pleasure and pain, such was my lifespan; and having died there, I reappeared elsewhere; and there too I was with such a name, of such a clan, with such an appearance; this was my food, such was my experience of pleasure and pain, such was my lifespan; and having died there, I reappeared here.’
Thus I recollected my many past lives in their forms and details.
This was the first true knowledge I attained in the first watch of the night. Ignorance was dispelled, true knowledge arose, darkness was dispelled, light arose, as happens in one who lives vigilant, ardent, and resolute.
Once my concentrated mind was purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilement, pliant, workable, steady, and imperturbable, I directed it to the knowledge of the passing away and reappearance of beings. With the divine eye, which is purified and surpasses the human, I saw beings passing away and reappearing, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, happily and unhappily reborn. I understood how beings continue according to their actions, thus:
‘These worthy beings, who lived badly in body, speech, and mind, reviled the noble ones, had wrong views, and let wrong views guide their actions, after the breakup of the body, after death, have reappeared in a state of misery, in a bad destination, in ruin, even in hell.
But these worthy beings, who lived well in body, speech, and mind, did not revile the noble ones, had right views, and let right views guide their actions, after the breakup of the body, after death, have reappeared in a good destination, even in the heavenly world.’
Thus with the divine eye, purified and surpassing the human, I saw beings passing away and reappearing, inferior and superior, beautiful and ugly, happily and unhappily reborn, and I understood how beings continue according to their actions.
This was the second true knowledge I attained in the middle watch of the night. Ignorance was dispelled, true knowledge arose, darkness was dispelled, light arose, as happens in one who lives vigilant, ardent, and resolute.
Once my concentrated mind was purified, bright, unblemished, rid of defilement, pliant, workable, steady, and imperturbable, I directed it to the knowledge of the destruction of the taints. I directly knew, as it really is:
‘This is suffering.’
‘This is the origin of suffering.’
‘This is the cessation of suffering.’
‘This is the path leading to the cessation of suffering.’
I directly knew, as it really is:
‘These are the taints.’
‘This is the origin of the taints.’
‘This is the cessation of the taints.’
‘This is the path leading to the cessation of the taints.’
As I thus knew and saw, my mind was liberated from the taint of sensuality, from the taint of becoming, and from the taint of ignorance. When it was liberated, the knowledge arose: ‘It is liberated.’
I directly knew: ‘Birth is ended, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more returning to any state of being.’
This was the third true knowledge I attained in the last watch of the night. Ignorance was dispelled, true knowledge arose, darkness was dispelled, light arose, as happens in one who lives vigilant, ardent, and resolute.
Now, brahmin, it might occur to you: ‘Perhaps the ascetic Gotama is still not free today from desire, hatred, and delusion, and that is why he still seeks secluded resting places in the jungle thickets.’
But you should not think in that way. For it is because I see two benefits that I still seek secluded resting places in the jungle thickets: I see a pleasant abiding for myself here and now, and I have compassion for future generations.”
